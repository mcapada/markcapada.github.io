SKILLS ONTARIO COMPETITION

First Place in Network Systems Administration - When I was invited to participate in this competition, I did not know what to expect because I had never heard of Skills Ontario before. The competition for Nationals was eight hours long and tested us on everything I had studied in my degree over the last three years (and more!). Long story short, competing was incredibly difficult and intimidating given that there are so many areas of knowledge involved. I managed to place First in Ontario for my respective field, which included an invitation to compete on the National Level.


SKILLS CANADA COMPETITION

Sixth Place in Canada - I had the opportunity to represent Team Ontario for the 2019 Skills Canada competition in Halifax, Nova Scotia. The contest scope included so much more in terms of areas of expertise for Network Systems Administration. Unlike the provincial competition where I was given a couple months to prepare, the Nationals only gave two weeks. The competition lasted 12 hours over the course of two days. At the end of the competition, I couldn't manage to get a podium placement, but I went through a life-changing experience. Looking back at it now, I had self-doubt in my abilities for the Skills Ontario competition, but I managed to place first. This shows me that even though I may show signs of uncertainty, I am still capable of achieving great things.
