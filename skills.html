SKILLS ONTARIO - MAY 2019

Best in Ontario for Network Systems Administration - The scope for the competition included various areas that were taught in post-secondary, but also covered areas that were too specific for some classes to cover. The three main areas in the provincial competition were Linux Server, Windows Server, and Routing and Switching. This included Windows Server 2016 administration involving ADDS, DHCP, and DNS. Cisco routers and switches were configured using various protocols (including OSPF and VLAN) and network addresses had to be planned and allocated correctly. Finally, Ubuntu Server CLI administration involved FTP, SSH, Apache2 and Samba.

SKILLS CANADA @ NOVA SCOTIA - 2019

Sixth in Canada for Network Systems Administration - The competition scope for the national level was similar to the provincial's scope but had higher emphasis on detail and accuracy. In addition to the tasks in Windows Server, implementations of RDP, GPO, and network monitoring had to be performed. Additionally, LDAP, DHCP, security management and RAID partitioning were expected of competitors in the Linux Server stage. The switched network provided was large scale and involved protocols such as NAT, Ether-Channel, ACLs, VPN Tunnels and Port Security in order to work.



FINAL THOUGHTS

I discovered a new-found confidence in my work and abilities - despite the feeling self-doubt, I can still come out successful. I won't ever forget the experience that I had, and I am eager to compete again for 2020. I was truly taken back by how big Skills, as an organization, really was. They are a global initiative and bring out the most talented of individuals.
