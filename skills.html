SKILLS 2019

"Even if you lose, at least you tried"




SKILLS ONTARIO - MAY 2019

Best in Ontario for Network Systems Administration - The scope for the competition was very vague with the intention of having competitors not know what to expect. The scope included various areas that were taught in post-secondary, but also had areas that are too specific for some classes to cover.

The four main areas in the provincial competition were Linux Server, Windows Server, Routing and Switching, and Network Planning/Connectivity. This included Windows Server 2016 CLI administration involving ADDS, DHCP, and DNS. Cisco routers and wwitches were configured using various protocols (including OSPF and VLAN) and network addresses had to be planned and allocated correctly. Finally, Ubuntu Server CLI administration involved FTP, SSH, Apache2 and Samba.


SKILLS CANADA @ NOVA SCOTIA - 2019

Sixth in Canada for Network Systems Administration - The competition scope for the national level was similar to the provincial's scope. However, there was a higher emphasis on detail and accuracy. In addition to the tasks in Windows Server, implementations of RDP, GPO, and network monitoring had to be performed. Additionally, LDAP, DHCP, security management and RAID partitioning were expected of competitors in the Linux Server stage. The switched network provided was large scale and involved protocols such as NAT, Ether-Channel, ACLs, VPN Tunnels and Port Security in order to work.


FINAL THOUGHTS

I never knew how big Skills really was - I had the opportunity to represent my college and province doing what I do best. I discovered a new-found confidence in my work and abilities - despite the feeling self-doubt, I can still come out successful. I won't ever forget this experience that I had, and I am eager to compete again for 2020. I was truly taken back by how big Skills, as an organization, really was. They are a global initiative and bring in the most talented of individuals. What I really enjoyed about my Skills Canada experience was being around the best in the province because they, too, probably believed that they couldn't even win in provincials.
